<h2>Module overview</h2>
<h3>Objectives</h3>
<p>This course provides an introduction to experimental research in psychology. The lecture component covers experimental design and statistics. The laboratory component focuses on designing and running experiments, applying statistical analyses to data generated first-hand in experiments, and reporting experimental findings. Lectures and labs are yoked together so that designs and analyses are introduced in lectures and then put into practice in labs.</p>
<h3>Outline Syllabus</h3>
<p>Topics include: experimental design, research ethics, and statistical analysis of experimental data up to and including the analysis of designs with one independent variable and more than two experimental conditions.</p>
<h3>Learning Outcomes</h3>
<p><span style="font-size: 0.9375rem;">By the end of this module, you should</span><br></p>
<ol>
    <li>Demonstrate an understanding of core statistical concepts;</li>
    <li>Choose an appropriate design/analysis for a psychological experiment;</li>
    <li>Carry out an appropriate analysis of experimental data using SPSS;</li>
    <li>Report experimental findings in a clear and concise way;</li>
    <li>Critically evaluate the design of existing experiments</li>
</ol>
